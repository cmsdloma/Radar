Wherever you lose signal, Radar finds a way

From post-disaster rubble to the inside of an aircraft, these are the situations where standard messaging apps go silent — and Radar keeps talking.

Natural Disaster & Grid Failure

An earthquake, flood, or major civil emergency can bring down cellular towers, knock out power, and sever fibre links in minutes. When the grid goes dark and every conventional communication channel fails, Radar operates entirely from the battery in your phone. WiFi Aware creates direct encrypted links between nearby devices. The mesh relay forwards messages through other Radar users, building a communication network from nothing but the people around you.

Onboard satellite internet costs a fortune and crawls along. But the ship runs its own closed WiFi network that connects every passenger and crew member on the same LAN. Radar discovers everyone on that network via mDNS and local scanning — free messaging, radar view, and group chat, all without using a single byte of expensive satellite data.

At 35,000 feet with phones in airplane mode, standard messaging is impossible. Many modern aircraft offer in-flight WiFi as a closed local network — no external internet access. Radar operates entirely within that LAN, letting passengers message each other, share their seat location on the radar view, and coordinate free of charge. No satellite subscription required.

Under the Hood

Three technologies, zero infrastructure

Radar stacks three complementary radio technologies so that if one isn't available in your environment, another takes over — seamlessly and automatically.

📡

WiFi Aware (NAN)

WiFi Aware lets two Android devices find each other and open an encrypted data socket directly — no router, no hotspot, no internet. This is Radar's primary messaging channel: sub-second discovery, high bandwidth, and completely peer-to-peer. Works on any Android 8.0+ device that supports the standard (most modern flagships and mid-range phones).

🏠

Isolated LAN (mDNS)

On any WiFi network — ship, hotel, aircraft, venue, office — Radar uses mDNS to announce and discover other Radar users on the same subnet, then opens direct TCP connections for messaging. The internet gateway can be down entirely or behind a paywall; Radar only needs the local network layer to work. Network scan also identifies all other devices on the LAN.

📲

Bluetooth LE Mesh

BLE runs continuously, even in the background, advertising your presence and scanning for other Radar users. It powers the radar view with RSSI-based distance estimation, and acts as a relay transport for the mesh when WiFi isn't available. BLE is the lowest-power, longest-lived radio in your pocket.

🔗

Store-and-Forward Mesh

Messages that can't be delivered immediately are stored and forwarded the next time a path opens. Each message carries a TTL (up to 5 hops), a unique ID for duplicate prevention, and loop-protection counters. This means a message can traverse a chain of devices to reach someone out of direct range — building an ad-hoc network from the people themselves.

🔒

End-to-End Security

WiFi Aware connections use PSK-secured data paths. Relay messages are encrypted at the transport layer. In local-only mode, no data ever leaves your device or network — there is nothing to intercept at a server because there is no server in the loop.

⚡

Background Operation

A persistent foreground service keeps BLE advertising and location broadcasting active when the app is minimised. You don't need to keep the screen on — Radar continues announcing your presence and accepting relay messages silently in the background, so your group always knows you're there.
